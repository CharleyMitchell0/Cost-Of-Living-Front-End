Debt Management Support

1. Mind yo’ own business

Being in debt can be overwhelming and it can be hard to see the light at the end of the tunnel if you have mounting bills to pay, especially during the cost-of-living challenges.

Richard Branson said it best, our first top tip is to ‘mind your own business’ and prioritise!

When thinking about and trying to get out of debt, make sure to focus on the most important things first.

Some great questions to ask yourself are do I have a debt consolidation action plan and do I understand my financial affairs?


2. Mind the electoral gap

Did you know that having your name on the electoral roll is usually seen as a sign of stability and dependability – and helps a finance firm confirm your identity and improve your credit rating.

If you’re currently a sofa surfer, you may have also dropped off the register. Can you reach out to friends and family to see if they can support you in getting added back on within their local authority?

You can also apply any time on the Government website; you’ll need your National Insurance number and local authority name to register. Don’t fall through the electoral gap!

3. Mind yo’ Ps fam!

Being declined for credit could be a useful wake-up call. Did you know that you can talk to us for free debt advice!

If you’re borrowing from the bank of mum and dad to pay off other debts, or because you don’t have enough money to pay your bills,

you should talk to one of our debt advisers. Find free advice through getting in touch using our cost-of-living support form

If you’ve been declined on affordability grounds, you may need to look at your finances as a whole and work out ways to save money. Please check out our budgeting and spending page.
