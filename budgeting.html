Budgeting & Spending Support

If giving up family meals out, your music subscription or your gym membership would make you miserable, don’t do it.

Your budget should help you see where else you can cut back. Then you can keep doing the things you love while living within your means.


Coupons, government grants and cost-cutting discounts could make a big difference to your life.

With the cost of living challenges and household bills on the rise, avoiding unnecessary costs has never been more important. Luckily, there’s a range of grants and discounts available to help you stretch your cash

The government have launched a support package to help vulnerable households. This included a £650 Cost of Living payment for those on benefits and a £300 pensioner Cost of Living payment.

Further payments are being made in 2023/4. To see if you are eligible, check the latest Goverment information.

Pet Care

If you have a pet that needs urgent treatment or you’re struggling to pay for its food, there are ways to get help.

For example, you might be able to claim Blue Cross vet care if you receive certain means-tested benefits and live within the catchment area of one of its hospitals or clinics.

Key Basics of budgeting

Breakdown your budget

The 50/20/30 budgeting rule is a popular one to help keep you right. It works by dividing up what money you have coming in and then breaking it down into how you’ll spend it:

50% on essentials, 30% on nice-to-haves, and 20% to savings, as a guide.
