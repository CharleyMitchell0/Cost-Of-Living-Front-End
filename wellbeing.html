Mental Health & Wellbeing Support

The cost-of-living challenges have significantly impacted mental health in the UK.

Here are some of our top tips for you to consider:

1. Learn new skills

Research shows that learning new skills can improve your mental health, help you feel positive and enhance your overall well-being.

This helps with boosting your self-confidence and building a sense of purpose.

Why not try out:

Cooking a new dish such as an Indian vegetable ruby murry or a Jamaican jerk chicken with rice and peas

Taking up a new hobby or interest such as playing pool, dungeons of dragon or learning a new instrument or language

Have you considered applying for a software development reskilling programme with your place of work?


2. Be Physically Active

That’s right. Press pause on the TV, the latest episode of GBBO or Strictly can wait

Getting out and about and being active is a great way for you to boost your health and fitness

It also helps you to be more productive in your day-to-day and can help to positively change your mood

Here are some helpful ideas:

Join a Park Run They’re free and a great way to socialise.

Take part in a charity fitness challenge

If you have a physical impairment, check out these fantastic fitness resources

OR train to be an Olympian for team GB

3.Get Connected

Good relationships are important for your mental wellbeing

They can help you to build a sense of belonging and self-worth, and provide emotional support whilst allowing you to support others

There are lots of things you could try to help build stronger and closer relationships:

Be intentional! Don’t wait for someone to reach out. Check up on friends and family or a neighbour

Why not volunteer at a local school, hospital or community group. Find out how to volunteer on the GOV.UK website

You can also visit a local place of worship or community hall for fun events and gatherings

Try switching off the TV to talk or play a game with your children, friends or family
